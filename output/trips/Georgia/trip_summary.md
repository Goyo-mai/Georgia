## Trip at a Glance

A 9-day adventure through three of Georgia's most dramatic regions in the coolest possible conditions for an August trip. Two nights in Tbilisi for history and city culture, three nights in Kazbegi at 1,740m elevation in the Greater Caucasus (temperature ~18–22°C vs. Tbilisi's 35°C), and three nights in the Kakheti wine country for food, wineries, and ancient monasteries. The group travels by 4WD rental SUV throughout, giving full flexibility on the Georgian Military Highway and mountain detours. Highlights include the iconic Gergeti Trinity Church hike beneath Mt Kazbek, the coolest experience of the trip inside Khareba Winery's 14°C mountain tunnel, and a traditional Georgian supra feast with qvevri wines.

## Key Facts

| | |
|-|-|
| Currency | Georgian Lari (GEL). $1 ≈ 2.7 GEL. Cards accepted in cities; bring cash for rural areas and marshrutkas. |
| Visa | Visa-free (up to 1 year) for US, EU, UK, Canada, Australia, NZ passports. |
| New 2026 Entry Rule | **Travel medical insurance is mandatory.** Minimum coverage: 30,000 GEL (~$11,000). Must be valid for entire stay. |
| Language | Georgian (unique 33-character script). English widely spoken in tourist areas and by younger Georgians. |
| Safety | Very safe — consistently rated safer than most Western European countries. Avoid South Ossetia and Abkhazia (conflict zones). Main risks: aggressive driving and mountain weather. |
| Emergency | 112 (police / fire / ambulance) |
| Tourist Helpline | +995 322 000 060 |
| August temperatures | Tbilisi: 34–37°C · Kazbegi: 18–22°C · Kakheti: 30–32°C · Gudauri Pass: ~15°C |
